What is photosensitive epilepsy?

Flashing lights at certain intensities or to certain visual patterns can trigger seizures.

Who is affected by photosensitive epilepsy?

Epilepsy is the fourth most common neurological disorder in the United States after migraine, stroke, and Alzheimer’s disease. About five percent of Americans can suffer from these epileptic triggers.

An estimated 70 percent of adults in the United States have experienced a traumatic event at least once in their lives and up to 20 percent of these people go on to develop posttraumatic stress disorder, or PTSD.
